# PRODUCT.md — AI Marketing Pulse

register: product

> A private intelligence feed for one reader. Curated AI-for-business tools, news,
> and people, rendered as a calm dated "issue" she can finish in the gaps between
> client calls. (Editorial lean: the reading experience matters as much as the tool.)

## Product purpose

AI Marketing Pulse turns the firehose of AI-for-business news into a small, finishable
daily issue. A pipeline collects candidates; a Claude Code session reads the real
articles and writes plain-language briefs; a React dashboard renders the latest issue
(a 30-second **Brief**, then sections), plus an archive. Scope is AI across business
functions (ops, finance, sales, support, marketing), tilted toward cost-saving /
automation / ROI signal. The product's job: make the reader credible with clients and
faster up her own learning curve, without ever feeling like a backlog.

## The one user

**Kajol Bheda** — Scribbld CEO moving into general AI consulting. A sharp marketer with
a designer's eye, high intelligence, and real creativity, tangled with anxiety and
boundless thoughts. Not a technical expert, but learning fast. She opens this feed in
tight gaps between client calls, on desktop and phone.

**Operational consequences (these are requirements, not vibes):**
- The page must never feel like a to-do list or an unread count. First glance must say
  *"this is small, warm, and finishable."*
- Depth exists but is opt-in. Every card stands alone: a plain-language summary, a
  one-line "why it matters" she can act on, a glossary for any jargon.
- Nothing urgent, nothing blinking, nothing red. Technical content is welcome;
  intimidating presentation of it is not.
- Reading flexibility is sacred: no view may constrain how she moves through an issue.
  The full feed is always one tap away.

## Brand — Scribbld "Highlighter & Ink"

Kajol's agency Scribbld brands in pure marker yellow (#FFD700) and black. The product
carries that as an *accent language*, never a repaint:
- Deep brand gold (`--accent`, #6e530e) for link text and markers.
- Marker yellow as decoration/surface only, never text: link underlines (the
  "highlighter" motif), the yellow sliver under ink rules (Scribbld's banner DNA
  miniaturized), and calmed surface derivatives like the butter Brief.
- Full-yellow floods and black bands stay on Scribbld's own properties. The reading
  surface stays warm paper.

The companion mascot is **RoboBuddy** — a presence, never a narrator. Its promise: she
is never alone with a wall of information, and nothing gets between her and reading her
own way.

## Tone & voice

Calm over clever. UI labels are one quiet word ("Details", "Less", "Issue"). No
exclamation marks in chrome. No urgency framing (no "don't miss", "act now", "new!",
no unread badges or counts). Action-phrased value ("pitch X to retail clients") over
clickbait. No em dashes in prose.

## Anti-references (what this must never become)

- A SaaS dashboard with hero-metric cards, unread badges, and a backlog feel.
- Dark mode / "AI purple" / neon / gradient washes. This is a paper product.
- Alarmist red anything, even errors (errors use deep gold).
- A dense wall of text, or a feed that gates how she reads.
- Generic news-aggregator chrome that could belong to any product.

## Strategic principles

1. **Finishable beats comprehensive.** Fewer, usable items win.
2. **Grounded or omitted.** Every claim traces to a real source; nothing invented.
3. **Opt-in depth.** Skim layer always visible; detail always behind one quiet step.
4. **Companion, not controller.** RoboBuddy reassures; it never instructs or blocks.
5. **One reader.** Every decision is judged against Kajol in a gap between calls.
